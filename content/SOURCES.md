# Grudge Map — Dalston sourcing starter kit

Starting points for verifiable facts. **Always open the source and confirm the
specific claim** before writing it into a fact pack — don't trust this list itself
as the source.

## Heritage listings (highest authority — `sourceKind: "historic-england"`)
- **Historic England — The List** (search "Dalston", "Hackney", "pillar box", "K2/K6 telephone box"): https://historicengland.org.uk/listing/the-list/
- Look specifically for: listed pillar boxes, K-series telephone kiosks, listed
  buildings on Kingsland High Street / Dalston Lane, and any listed ironwork.

## Postal / street-furniture heritage (`other` / `archive`)
- **Letter Box Study Group** — cipher dating (VR = Victoria Regina), box types.
- **Royal Mail postal heritage** — first pillar boxes (1852–53), original green paint.
- London street-furniture / bollard history (debunk the "Waterloo cannon" myth).

## Local history + archives (`archive` / `local-press`)
- **Hackney Archives** (London Borough of Hackney).
- **Layers of London** — mapped historical layers: https://www.layersoflondon.org/
- **Britain from Above** — aerial photos of Dalston across the 20th century.
- **Hackney Society** / local plaques.
- **Hackney Gazette** archive for the pub → coffee-shop / market change stories.

## Specific Dalston places (name-drop material — `wikidata` / `local-press`)
- **Ridley Road Market** — trading since the 1880s; official Hackney market page.
- **Gillett Square** / **Bradbury Street** — Hackney Co-operative Developments regeneration.
- **Café OTO**, Ashwin Street — opened 2008 in a former print works.
- **Dalston Eastern Curve Garden** — on the disused Broad Street railway curve.
- **Dalston Kingsland** & **Dalston Junction** stations — competing Victorian railways;
  Broad Street branch closed 1986.
- **Kingsland Road** — on the line of the Roman **Ermine Street**.

## For Edith's "price of everything" grievance
- Find the **actual pub** on/near Dalston Lane that is now a specialty coffee shop.
  Name both, and find a period price vs a current price (a pint in the 1970s vs a
  flat white now). Hackney Gazette / local blogs / the venue's own history page.

## Tone references (not facts — for voice, PRD §5)
- The humour is character + status mismatch + a true fact used as a setup.
- Read finished lines aloud. If it sounds like a plaque or a tour guide, rewrite it.
